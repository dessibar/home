Project name:

Decision Support Tools for Sustainable Management of Capelin Stocks in Icelandic and Barents Sea Ecosystems (DESSIBAR) - Principal Investigator: Subbey, Samuel

Project Focus:

Capelin in the Barents Sea and around Iceland

Funding and Duration:

The project will be funded by the Nordic Council of Ministers (151-2016-DESSIBAR) over 3 years (2017-2019)

Thematics and Research Goals:

The project has two main thematic subjects namely, projecting scientific survey estimates to spawning stock biomasses, and setting sustainable harvest target limits under uncertainty. The project will identify, compare and contrast key ecosystem traits that act as capelin dynamics drivers, and how these have changed over time. It will develop improved monitoring techniques, stock projection models, and management decision support tools. Finally, DESSIBAR will seek to develop end-user interface to the tools developed so as to make them easily accessible to the ICES community as a whole.

Project overarching goal

To ensure sustainable harvest of the capelin stocks through pooling research resources to develop common/shared tools for monitoring, stock projection and target rules for the capelin stock

Work packages

  • WP1: Establishment of dynamic database - Led by Gumundsdottir, Asta
  • WP2: Correlating ecosystem characteristics - Led by Gjøsæter, Harald
  • WP3: Tools for stock projection and advice - Led by Subbey, Samuel
  • WP4: Interface and dissemination tools - Led by Lamo, Yngve
